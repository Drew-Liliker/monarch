Monarch Butterflies

Monarch Butterflies are a type of butterfly.

Scientific Name: Danaus plexippus

Habitat and Area

Monarch Butterflies live near North America. Their habitat includes prairies, meadows, grasslands and along roadsides

Monarch Butterfly eggs usually hatch about 4 days after being laid. They usually have around 300-400 eggs.

Population

Today, Monarch Butterflies are on the endangered list. It faces its threats from habitat destruction to climate change. About 250,000 are left today in 2022, increasing from 1,914 in 2021.

Why?

More are dying because Monarchs have many natural enemies. Predators such as spiders and fire ants kill and eat monarch eggs and caterpillars. Some birds and wasps kill the adult butterfly.

How to help?

2 ways to stop them from extinction includes to stop using pesticides, and to plant native-milkweed, which they eat.
